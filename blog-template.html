Developing structured sections

Use clear headings to guide readers through each stage of your story or argument. Strong hierarchy helps with scanning and keeps the layout feeling intentional.

Why headings matter

Headings break the page into manageable pieces. They also help retain context as readers scroll through longer material.

What to include in a list

Bullet points can help summarize main ideas without forcing readers to wade through dense paragraphs.

  • Keep each item short and specific.
  • Use parallel grammar for better flow.
  • Reserve bullets for key details or steps.

Quest notes

When designing the quest, I had imagined a ledger to keep track of which characters currently held which tasks, which items were checked-in and checked-out, etc... However, I ran out of time to think too much about this problem and ended up with an ad-hoc sheet of paper, which did work well enough that I could pair up "export" and "import" quests, and have characters collect items which had been placed by other players.

The complete lack of structure was a big source of stress while running the game though.
